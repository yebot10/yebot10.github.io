Do opioid overdoses tend to be associated with less affluent areas — that is, areas where families have lower incomes?¶

In order to answer this question I decided to use a scatter plot. This graph seemed like an effective tool in order to compared two numeric data fields, such as household income versus the opioid overdose rate.

In [104]:
fig,ax = plt.subplots()
ax.scatter(opioidsVA['MedianHouseholdIncome'], opioidsVA['FPOO-Rate'], c = 'purple')
ax.xaxis.set_label_text('Median Household Income')
ax.yaxis.set_label_text('FPOO-Rate / Opioid OD per 100,000')
fig.suptitle('Does Median Household Income in Virginia Influence Opiod OD Rates?')
plt.show()

Which states are associated with the greatest number of United States Presidents in terms of the Presidents’ birthplaces?¶

To display the data needed to answer the above question in the most concise and effective manner, I decided to employ the use of the bar graph. I also limited my bar graph to only contain the top 5 states since the question being asked only concerns which states birthed the "greatest number" of U.S. Presidents, not the total number for every state.

In [184]:
PresStates = {}
for i in presidents['State']:
    if i in PresStates:
        PresStates[i] += 1
    else: 
        PresStates[i] = 1
PresStates = sorted(PresStates.items(), key = lambda president: president[1], reverse = True)
states,num_pres = zip(*PresStates)
